WELCOME

SwifNet offers a core messaging service (FIN) which standardizes message types between the members of its network. Companies can use this messaging service to transmit data back and forth with their banks. Common message types include MT103 (credit transfer), MT940 (account statement), and MX pain. SWIFT payments work by facilitating secure, standardized communication between banks for international money transfers.

Our Services

Initiation

When someone initiates a SWIFT transfer, their bank (the sender's bank) sends a payment instruction message.

Messaging

This message contains details like the sender's and recipient's bank details, the amount to be transferred, and the currency.

Routing

The SWIFT network then routes this message to the recipient's bank.

Execution

The recipient's bank receives the message and uses it to debit the sender's account and credit the recipient's account.

Intermediary Banks

In some cases, intermediary banks might be involved to facilitate the transfer between the sender's and recipient's banks.

Confirmation

Once the transfer is complete, the recipient's bank sends a confirmation message back through the SWIFT network.
